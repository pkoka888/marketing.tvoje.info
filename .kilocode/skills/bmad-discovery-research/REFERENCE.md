# Discovery Research Reference Guide

## Research Methods

### Primary Research Techniques

| Method | Best For | Duration | Output |
|--------|----------|----------|--------|
| User Interviews | Deep insights, pain points | 30-60 min/person | Transcripts, quotes |
| Surveys | Quantitative data, broad reach | 1-2 weeks | Statistics, trends |
| Contextual Inquiry | Real behavior observation | 2-4 hours | Journey maps |
| Competitive Audit | Feature comparison | 1-2 days | Matrix, reports |
| Domain Expert Interview | Technical validation | 45-90 min | Validation notes |

### Secondary Research Sources

| Source Type | Examples | Reliability |
|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Academic | Google Scholar, IEEE | High |
| Industry Reports | Gartner, Forrester | Medium-High |
| Company Blogs | Engineering blogs, case studies | Medium |
| Open Source | GitHub, GitLab | Varies |
| Community | Reddit, Hacker News, Discord | Low-Medium |

## Competitor Analysis Framework

### SWOT Analysis Template

```markdown
## Competitor: [Name]

### Strengths
- What they do well
- Unique features
- Market position

### Weaknesses
- Gaps in offering
- Customer complaints
- Technical limitations

### Opportunities
- Market trends they ignore
- Underserved segments
- Technology shifts

### Threats
- Emerging competitors
- Regulatory changes
- Market disruption
```

## Citation Format

### Source Citation Template

```
[Number] Source: [Title], [Author], [Publisher], [Date], [URL]
```

Example:
```
[1] "State of DevOps 2023", DORA Research Program, Google, 2023, https://services.google.com/fh/files/misc/state-of-devops-2023.pdf
```
