# Story Planning Checklist

## Prerequisites
- [ ] Epics exist and are approved (from bmad-product-planning)
- [ ] Architecture decisions are documented and stable
- [ ] UX assets (wireframes, prototypes) are available when relevant
- [ ] Sprint capacity and delivery cadence are defined

## Story Creation
- [ ] Each story has a clear, user-centric title
- [ ] Story includes acceptance criteria that are testable
- [ ] Dependencies are explicitly listed
- [ ] Prerequisites are documented
- [ ] Story references upstream epic and architecture decisions
- [ ] Test hooks are defined (unit, integration, e2e)
- [ ] Delivery signals are specified (what "done" looks like)

## Backlog Management
- [ ] Stories are prioritized based on value, dependencies, and risk
- [ ] Dependencies between stories are mapped
- [ ] Blockers are identified and communicated
- [ ] Sprint sequencing is logical and feasible
- [ ] Capacity estimates are realistic

## Quality Gates
- [ ] All stories align with architecture guardrails
- [ ] No story violates established technical decisions
- [ ] Acceptance criteria are unambiguous
- [ ] Each story can be completed independently (or with clear dependencies)
- [ ] Stories are sized appropriately for sprint capacity

## Documentation
- [ ] Story files follow the template structure
- [ ] Backlog summary is up to date
- [ ] Sprint status is documented
- [ ] Next-step recommendations are clear

## Handoff
- [ ] Stories are ready for development-execution
- [ ] Development team has reviewed and accepted stories
- [ ] Test team has reviewed acceptance criteria
- [ ] Stakeholders are informed of sprint plan
